What is Crochet?

Crochet is a type of needlecraft which involves the use of a hooked needle to create fabric from loops of yarn or threads. It is similar to knitting. But instead of using 2 needles, crochet uses a single hook. With crochet, you can create a wide variety of items such as blankets, hats,and even intricate lacework. It's a hobby enjoyed by many people around the world which offers a fun, creative way to spend your free time.





History and Origin of Crochet

Much like knitting, the history of crochet is shrouded in mystery and remains largely undocumented. Historians do not know who thecreator of crochet is or the exact place where it originated. What we consider to be crochet today can be traced back to the 15th-17th century. Many Cultures have claimed their crochet history including: Italy, Arabia, and Chuna. But the true homeland of crochet remains unknown.


Crochet came from the French word Croc/Croche which means hook. It also could be connected to the Norse word krokr, which also means hook.





Materials and Tools

Crocheting can require various types of tools and equipment that can improve the quality of our work. As a beginner we will only focus on the most essential basic crochet materials which consist of the Yarn, Crochet hooks, Scissors, Tapestry needle, and Stitch Markers.


1. Yarn

This is the material we'll be crocheting with. This will depend on the person's project, it has different colours and types such as wool or cotton that can add to create diverse textures to theircreations.




2. Crochet Hooks

This is the tool that we'll need to loop the yarn. This come's in various sizes and materials

like metal or wood which can add different textures and feeling in our hand while crocheting.




3. Scissors

This will be used to cut and lessen the risk of ruining the yarns' quality.




4. Tapestry Needle

Helps to weave the loose ends. A tapestry needle is a blunt needle with a large eye. The largeeye is useful to needleworkers because it can accommodate threads or fibres that are thicker than ordinary sewing thread. Most tapestry needles are large enough to accommodate crochet thread or embroidery floss, and many are large enough to accommodate yarn as well.




5. Stitch markers

Stitch markers can help to track your progress. It is a small tool that helps your track of what round, row or stitch you're on in your crochet project.







Process of Learning Crochet

First is to know how to hold a hook which can either be Pencil grip or Knife grip. Remember that It's important to learn how to hold your yarn because of tension. Tension is important in crochet because it affects the overall appearance and size of your project.


There are a lot of stitches that we can learn in order to progress this include the Two basic knots called Slip knot and Chain Stitch. After learning the Two basics knot we can now move to the stitches called Single Crochet (SC), Double Crochet (DC), and finally the Finishing.
